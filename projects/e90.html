BMW E90 Performance Platform

A ground-up motorsport build covering exhaust fabrication, thermal management system design, fluid system engineering, and full electrical integration — all self-designed in SolidWorks and fabricated in my personal shop.

Timeline 2024 — Ongoing
Role Design, Fabrication, Integration
Tools SolidWorks, TIG, Bridgeport

Exhaust System

Designed and TIG welded custom downpipes and a full exhaust system. Downpipes and turbo hot side were sent to Swaintech for ceramic thermal barrier coating to manage underhood heat. Fabricated replacement heat shields from 3003 aluminum with gold reflective heat tape for additional thermal protection of adjacent components.

Thermal & Fluid Systems

Engineered a dual oil cooler setup with custom stainless steel AN line routing and integrated drain. Designed a differential cooler system (in progress) with a modified diff cover featuring a drain plug and fluid temperature sensor port. Manufactured custom adapter fittings to convert the vacuum pump and throttle body to metric AN port connections.

Mechanical Fabrication

Designed and fabricated mounting brackets for dual 0.5L catch cans in the space previously occupied by the power steering reservoir. Redesigned the boost solenoid mount for reduced weight and improved thermal isolation. Created engine protection plates to cover exposed ports during the engine-out bearing replacement service.

Electrical Integration

Replaced OEM seats with Recaros and coded the relevant control modules to suit. Removed unnecessary telematics wiring and modules to reduce weight and complexity. Began tying into unused ports on the battery power distribution block to support a trunk-mounted electric power steering pump and future auxiliary pumps.
