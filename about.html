About

Engineer, machinist, fabricator,
and builder of things.

I'm a mechanical engineer based in southern New Hampshire with a background in manufacturing, field engineering, and aerospace repair — and a personal shop where I spend most of my free time designing and building.

01

Background

I graduated from the University of Washington in 2019 with a B.S. in Applied Physics. While at UW, I spent three years on the Formula SAE team — first on manufacturing, then suspension design, and finally as Race Team Lead — and worked as a student machinist in the ME Research Lab.

Professionally, I've worked across manufacturing engineering, field service and technical support for automated systems, aerospace component repair, and mechanical design for custom automotive chassis. I've written service manuals, trained teams, developed repair strategies for aerospace hardware, and built SolidWorks API macros to automate engineering workflows.

Outside of work, I run a personal shop focused on motorsport vehicle builds, wiring harness fabrication, PCB design, and anything else that involves turning a design into physical hardware. My approach is the same whether I'm at work or at home: understand the problem, design a solution, build it, test it, and iterate.
