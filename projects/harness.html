Motorsport Wiring Harnesses

Mil-spec methodology harness fabrication for motorsport applications. Every harness is built to withstand vibration, heat, and moisture using materials and techniques consistent with aerospace and military standards.

Application Motorsport / Automotive
Crimp Tooling DMC MH860
Connectors DTM, Superseal, Autosport coming soon

Materials & Methods

All harnesses use Tefzel wire (typically 18–22 AWG, all white with identification loops at the ends) with solid barrel pins crimped using a DMC MH860 crimp frame with appropriate positioners. The primary connector family is Deutsch DTM for its vibration resistance and environmental sealing, with Bosch and pressure transducer-specific connectors used where required by sensors. Flying leads are used in select applications.

Construction

Exposed sections of the harness are concentric twisted and protected with Raychem DR-25 heat shrink and sealed boots. Straight transitions use SCL or ATUM depending on the application. Interior harness branches receive either Raychem or Techflex braided sleeving. Every harness incorporates service loops at connectors and branch points for strain relief and future serviceability.

Why This Matters

The materials and methods used here — Tefzel wire, concentric twist, Raychem heat shrink, solid barrel crimps with calibrated tooling, and service loops — are the same foundational techniques used in aerospace and military harness fabrication. The difference is the application, not the standard of workmanship.
