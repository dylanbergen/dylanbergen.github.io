Miata ITB & Standalone ECU

Adapted motorcycle individual throttle bodies to a Miata engine by machining the stock intake manifold on a Bridgeport and TIG welding the throttle bodies in place. Integrated a Megasquirt 2 standalone ECU with a custom sensor harness and performed both real-time and data-based engine tuning.

Platform Mazda Miata
ECU Megasquirt 2
Disciplines Machining, Welding, Calibration

Intake Manifold Fabrication

The stock Miata intake manifold was machined on a Bridgeport mill to accept motorcycle individual throttle bodies. The throttle bodies were then TIG welded to the machined manifold, creating a bolt-on ITB setup using the factory mounting points.

ECU Integration

The Megasquirt 2 was a plug-and-play base unit, but the ITB conversion required additional sensors. I fabricated a custom wiring harness for a throttle position sensor, manifold air temperature sensor, and wideband O2 sensor — integrating them into the existing engine harness.

Calibration

Engine tuning was done through a combination of real-time adjustment (live on the car) and data-based tuning using logged sensor data. This involved building fuel and ignition maps from scratch to match the new intake configuration, targeting both drivability and performance across the full RPM range.

Data Acquisition

Experience with AiM and MoTeC data acquisition systems for motorsport data collection and analysis, including logging and review of engine parameters, chassis data, and driver inputs.
